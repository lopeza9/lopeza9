Muay Thai & Kickboxing

For those who aren't familiar with these two types of martial arts, there are similarities between Muay Thai and Kickboxing. Muay Thai and Kickboxing are an 8-point striking type of combat. Also known as "The Art of 8 Limbs," they use both hands and feet along with elbows and knees. Muay Thai training requires a tremendous amount of power in its movements, along with the focus and discipline that any other martial art requires.

Muay Thai is considered to be a full-body workout and one of the most effective styles of fighting. This martial art first originated in Thailand back in the 13th century. It was made for the soldiers to defend their kingdom as a type of up-close combat. Invented by Nai Khanom Tom, often referred to as "The Father of Muay Thai Training".

Benefits Of Muay Thai & Kickboxing

The physical benefits of Muay Thai and Kickboxing include toning and strengthening your body and core. Along with getting rid of stubborn fat and improving your flexibility. Muay Thai is a great way to tone your entire body, as it's considered a full-body workout. Not only will it strengthen your body, but it also improves mobility and cardiovascular conditions as well, which can help you get into the best shape of your life.

For the mental benefits of Muay Thai and Kickboxing, you will learn new tools to use to help you stay calm. Many athletes notice that they start thinking strategically and gain confidence as they train and practice more and more. With this type of mental training, you will even experience a boost in mood, which is a direct result of increasing endorphins to help relieve stress, depression, and anxiety! In other words, combat sports like Muay Thai or Kickboxing build mental toughness and self-discipline, as well as physical toughness.
